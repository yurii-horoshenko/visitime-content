I moved from Ukraine to Poland two years ago. It took me three weeks to find a barber who speaks my language.

Three weeks of Instagram DMs. WhatsApp messages. Phone calls in Polish I barely speak. One barber said yes, then ghosted. Another booked me, then cancelled in a voice note I couldn't fully understand.

When someone is holding scissors near your head, "we kind of understand each other" is not good enough.

So I opened every booking app on the App Store. Filters for price. Filters for rating. Filters for distance. Zero filters for language. Zero filters for gender.

That's the gap. Not beauty. Not scheduling. Language and gender — the two things that actually decide whether a service works for a real human.

I'm building VisiTime to close it.

A booking app for any service — haircut, nails, therapy, a tutor for my kid — where you filter by the things that matter. Language you speak. Gender of the master. Location. Price. Then book a slot, pay, show up.

iOS and Android, one Kotlin Multiplatform codebase, .NET backend. Solo founder. 13 AI agents on my team — more on that this week.

I'm going to document every step here. The wins, the bugs, the months I'll burn on things that don't work. Week 1 is the "why" and the "who."
