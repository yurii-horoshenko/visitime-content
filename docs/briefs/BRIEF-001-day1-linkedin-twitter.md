## POST #1-A: LinkedIn

Three hours. That's how long it took me to find a Ukrainian-speaking barber in Warsaw.

Not because there aren't any — there are hundreds. But they're buried in Telegram groups and word-of-mouth that assumes you already know the right people.

I moved from Ukraine to Poland a couple of years ago. One of the small, invisible things nobody warns you about: finding a barber feels genuinely hard. Not because of the haircut. Because of the language. Because of the comfort of knowing someone understands you — your culture, your references, the way you say "shorter but not too short."

That was the moment I started building VisiTime.

The problem isn't Ukrainian immigrants in Poland. That's just where I felt it. The same friction exists for Spanish speakers in Germany, Arabic speakers in France, Korean speakers in Canada. Any time language or culture matters for a personal service — and it almost always does — the current booking experience fails people.

VisiTime is a service booking app for clients who want to filter by language, gender, and availability. And for businesses that want to actually manage their schedules without duct-taping three tools together.

I'm building it solo. iOS + Android + backend. And I'm building it publicly — sharing every decision, every tradeoff, every mistake.

Building this solo — but not alone. I have 13 AI agents on my team. More on that this week.

If that kind of journey is something you want to follow, hit Follow.

Day 1 starts now.
